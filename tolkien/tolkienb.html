BAG END

The Hobbit Hole (smial) and ancestral home of the Baggins family of Hobbiton in the Shire. Bag End was the original name for Tolkien's aunt Jane Neave's Dormston Manor Farm, just a few miles from the author's childhood home in the hamlet of Sarehole in what was then rural Worcestershire. The Manor's orgins stretch back to Anglo-Saxon times, and it undoubtedly fired Toklien's imagination in his construction of the fictional world of Middle-Earth. However, it was perhaps more than just his childhood memories that Tolkien drew on in his creation of Bag End.

I came from the end of a bag, but no bag went over me - Bilbo Baggins riddles of Bag End in his contest of wits with the dragon of Erebor.

Just like the Hobbit, his creator was fascinated with puns, word games, and riddles, and by literary sleights of hand. Futhermore, there are elements of social satire in the name Bag End . As the eminent Tolkien scolar Tom Shipley has observed, Bag End is a literal translation of the French cul de sac, a term employed by snobbish British real estate agents in the early twentieth century who felt the English term "Dead-End-Road" too vulgar. Naturally, Tolkien's very English Baggins family would have no truck with this kind of Francophiled silliness, and so decided upon this suitably authentic local English name of Bag End.
